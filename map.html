Compare and contrast language spoken by Presidents in the State of the Union

This site lets you use a Bookworm database to compare the language spoken by every president in his State of the Union addresses.

In this view, a technique called "Named Entity Recognition" has been used to pull every place ever mentioned from all the speeches. By clicking on a spot, you'll see a list of the most statistically significant words associated with that place in presidential rhetoric.

In later versions, you'll be able to drill even further to limit the map by time, and to bring up the particular speeches in which a word was used.

Keep in mind that the State of the Union has changed a lot in its history. Of particular importance to understanding these charts is knowing that it was written, not spoken, before Woodrow Wilson.
